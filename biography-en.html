Biography

The life story of Ihor Utyuzh — a path of strength, dignity, loyalty, and love.

Childhood & Character

From early childhood, Ihor was active, energetic, and curious. His parents recall him as a boy who “always found something to do,” with a natural determination and a strong will.

He practiced martial arts, rowing, and sports, and had a rare ability to connect with people. A few words, a smile — and he was already “one of us.”

Revolution of Dignity

At 17, Ihor made a choice that shaped his life. He joined the 24th Self-Defense Sotnia of Maidan, guarded barricades, and helped evacuate the wounded during clashes with Berkut.

On February 18, 2014, he was severely injured in Mariinsky Park — part of his skull was shattered. Doctors saved his life after several emergency surgeries.

Later he said a phrase that became symbolic:
“If my life was saved, it means I’m needed for something greater.”

Return to Service

Despite the severe injury, Ihor recovered and later joined the Anti-Terrorist Operation. In 2016 he received the medal “For Sacrifice and Love for Ukraine.”

Months before the full-scale invasion, he told his family:
“If it starts — I will go.”

On February 24, 2022, while abroad, he immediately began his journey back to Ukraine.

Air Assault Forces

Ihor served in the 78th Separate Air Assault Regiment as a UAV operator. He worked on difficult frontlines, supported his comrades, and never left anyone behind.

His callsign “Slon” (“Elephant”) reflected his strength, endurance, and reliability. As a child he once told his father:
“When I grow up, I’ll go to Africa to protect elephants.”

Last Battle & Legacy

Ihor fought in the liberation of Kyiv and Kharkiv regions, and later on the Zaporizhzhia and Kherson fronts. In December 2023, during a combat mission, an enemy shell struck his dugout.

He dreamed of victory, of traveling to Crimea, of creating a charitable foundation. He loved life — and fought so that others could live.

Thank you, Elephant. Eternal memory to the Hero.
